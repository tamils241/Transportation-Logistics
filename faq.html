Customs Questions

Required documents typically include: commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading (ocean) or airway bill (air), certificate of origin, export declaration (AES/EEI for US exports), import license (if applicable), phytosanitary certificates (for agricultural products), MSDS sheets (for chemicals), and insurance certificates. Our customs brokerage team will guide you through all requirements specific to your shipment's origin, destination, and cargo type.

Customs clearance times vary by country and shipment type. For standard shipments with complete documentation: US customs typically clears within 1-2 business days, EU countries within 1-3 business days, and other major trading partners within 2-5 business days. Shipments requiring inspections, permits, or additional documentation may take longer. Our customs pre-clearance process and electronic documentation submission help minimize delays significantly compared to manual filings.

Customs duties and taxes are determined by the destination country's tariff schedule and are the responsibility of the importer of record. We offer DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) and DAP (Delivered at Place) incoterms to clarify responsibility. Our customs team can provide duty estimates before shipping, help classify your goods using correct HS codes, and advise on duty reduction strategies such as free trade agreements and bonded warehousing. We can also prepay duties on your behalf for seamless, hassle-free delivery.
